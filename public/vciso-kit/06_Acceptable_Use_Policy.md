# Acceptable Use Policy

**[ORGANIZATION_NAME]**

---

## 1. Purpose

Define acceptable and prohibited use of organizational information systems, networks, and equipment.

---

## 2. Scope

All users of organizational IT resources, including employees, contractors, and guests.

---

## 3. Acceptable Uses

Users may use systems for:
- Job-related work
- Authorized professional development
- Limited personal use (reasonable, non-disruptive)
- Emergency communications

---

## 4. Prohibited Uses

### 4.1 Illegal Activities
- Unauthorized access to systems
- Distribution of malware
- Hacking attempts
- Copyright/intellectual property violations
- Fraud or deception

### 4.2 Harmful Activities
- Harassment or threatening behavior
- Defamation or libel
- Discrimination
- Sexual harassment
- Violence or harm threats

### 4.3 Resource Abuse
- Excessive personal use
- Downloading illegal content
- Running competing businesses
- Cryptocurrency mining
- Spam or unsolicited communications

### 4.4 Data Misuse
- Sharing passwords
- Accessing others' files without authorization
- Copying confidential data
- Selling organizational data
- Unauthorized data transfer

### 4.5 Security Violations
- Circumventing security controls
- Disabling antivirus/firewall
- Sharing accounts
- Leaving systems unattended without lock
- Connecting unauthorized devices

### 4.6 Inappropriate Content
- Adult/pornographic material
- Gambling sites
- Pirated software
- Illegal content
- Hate speech

---

## 5. Monitoring and Enforcement

### 5.1 Monitoring Activities
- Email monitoring (with appropriate notice)
- Website activity monitoring
- File access logging
- Network traffic analysis

### 5.2 Privacy Expectations
- No expectation of privacy in business communications
- Personal communications more protected (but still monitored)
- Data subject to discovery in legal cases
- Monitoring disclosed in employment agreements

### 5.3 Enforcement Actions
- First offense: Warning and training
- Repeated violation: Suspension of access
- Serious violation: Termination of employment
- Legal action if applicable

---

## 6. Mobile Device Use

### 6.1 Approved Personal Devices
- Business review required
- Device must meet security standards
- Mobile device management enrolled
- Remote wipe capability enabled

### 6.2 Authorized Applications
- Only approved apps permitted
- No jailbreaking/rooting
- Automatic updates enabled
- Strong passcode required

---

## 7. Software and Licensing

- Only authorized software installed
- Licensed software tracked
- Open source code reviewed
- Software audits conducted
- Unlicensed software prohibited

---

## 8. Cloud Services

- Approved cloud services only
- Data classification controls
- Business review required
- Encryption of sensitive data
- Terms of service reviewed

---

## 9. Social Media

### 9.1 Work-Related Posting
- Company approval required
- Official communications vetted
- Brand protection maintained
- Accurate information only

### 9.2 Personal Social Media
- No disclosure of confidential information
- No disparagement of company
- Professional conduct maintained
- Personal account, company consequences

---

## 10. Bring Your Own Device (BYOD)

### 10.1 Requirements
- Personal device provided by user
- Mobile device management installed
- Strong authentication enabled
- Data encryption required
- Remote wipe permitted

### 10.2 Acceptable Use
- Business applications only
- Approved cloud services
- VPN required for network access
- No personal storage of sensitive data

---

## 11. Violations and Consequences

**Minor Violations:**
- Written warning
- Mandatory training
- Monitoring period

**Serious Violations:**
- Suspension of system access
- Investigation conducted
- Potential termination

**Policy Agreement:**
- All users must acknowledge policy
- Compliance mandatory
- Violations reported to management

---

## 12. Exceptions and Appeals

- Request in writing to CISO
- Business justification required
- Time-limited exceptions (max 30 days)
- Review and renewal required
- Non-compliance escalated
