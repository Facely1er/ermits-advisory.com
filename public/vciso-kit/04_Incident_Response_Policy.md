# Incident Response Policy

**[ORGANIZATION_NAME]**

---

## 1. Purpose

Establish framework for detecting, responding to, and recovering from security incidents to minimize impact and restore operations.

---

## 2. Scope

All security incidents including: unauthorized access, data breaches, malware, denial of service, system compromise, policy violations.

---

## 3. Incident Severity Levels

### Level 1 (Critical)
- Widespread system impact
- Sensitive data compromised
- Regulatory notification required
- Immediate response (within 1 hour)
- Escalate to Board

### Level 2 (High)
- Limited system impact
- Potential data exposure
- Response within 4 hours
- Executive notification

### Level 3 (Medium)
- Isolated system
- Minor data risk
- Response within 24 hours
- Department notification

### Level 4 (Low)
- Information only
- No data risk
- Response within 1 week
- Team lead notification

---

## 6. Response Process

### Phase 1: Detection & Analysis (0-4 hours)
- Verify incident occurred
- Gather initial information
- Assess severity
- Activate response team
- Begin documentation

### Phase 2: Containment (Within severity timeframe)
- Isolate affected systems
- Prevent further access
- Preserve evidence
- Backup critical data
- Implement temporary mitigations

### Phase 3: Investigation (Ongoing)
- Determine root cause
- Identify scope
- Collect forensic evidence
- Trace attacker (if applicable)
- Document findings

### Phase 4: Eradication
- Remove malware/attacker access
- Patch vulnerabilities
- Reset compromised credentials
- Restore from clean backups
- Verify remediation

### Phase 5: Recovery
- Restore systems to production
- Monitor for re-compromise
- Restore from backups
- Rebuild if necessary
- Validate functionality

### Phase 6: Post-Incident
- Lessons learned meeting
- Process improvements
- Staff training
- Documentation complete
- Regulatory reporting (if needed)

---

## 7. Communication

### 7.1 Internal Communications
- Immediate: executives and affected departments
- Hourly: status updates (if critical)
- Daily: progress reports
- Completion: final briefing

### 7.2 External Communications
- Customer notification (if data exposed)
- Regulatory authority notification (if required)
- Media statement (if public disclosure)
- All communications reviewed by legal

---

## 8. Evidence Preservation

### 8.1 Chain of Custody
- Evidence logged and tracked
- Access restricted
- Movement documented
- No modification or contamination

### 8.2 Forensics
- Forensic specialist engaged immediately
- Systems imaged before cleanup
- Analysis conducted on forensic copy
- Original evidence preserved

---

## 9. Notification Obligations

### 9.1 Breach Notification (if applicable)
- GDPR: Within 72 hours to authority
- CCPA: Without unreasonable delay
- State laws: Per specific requirements
- Notification content: Nature, scope, measures

### 9.2 Regulatory Notification
- Cyber insurance provider
- Relevant authorities
- Industry regulators
- All within specified timeframes

---

## 10. Recovery Time Objectives

- **Critical Systems:** 4 hours RTO
- **Important Systems:** 24 hours RTO
- **Standard Systems:** 72 hours RTO
- **Test systems:** 1 week RTO

---

## 11. Testing & Drills

- Incident response drills quarterly
- Full-scale exercises annually
- Tabletop exercises semi-annually
- Plan updated based on results

---

## 12. Post-Incident Review

Within 14 days of incident conclusion:
- Root cause analysis
- Prevention measures
- Process improvements
- Staff training needs
- Documentation complete
