# Third-Party Risk Management Policy

**[ORGANIZATION_NAME]**

---

## 1. Purpose

Manage security risks from third-party vendors, partners, and service providers with system or data access.

---

## 2. Scope

All third parties with: system access, data access, business-critical functions, network connectivity.

---

## 3. Third-Party Assessment

### 3.1 Pre-Engagement Assessment
Before engagement, third parties must demonstrate:
- Security controls in place
- Certifications (SOC 2, ISO 27001, etc.)
- Incident response capability
- Data protection practices
- Cyber insurance coverage

### 3.2 Assessment Questionnaire
Detailed security questionnaire covering:
- Security policies and procedures
- Access control measures
- Encryption practices
- Incident response plan
- Business continuity capability
- Audit history

### 3.3 On-Site Assessment
For high-risk vendors:
- Security control review
- Physical security inspection
- Staff interviews
- Documentation review
- Testing of controls

---

## 4. Contractual Requirements

### 4.1 Security Clauses
All contracts must include:
- Security requirements
- Data protection obligations
- Incident notification (24-48 hours)
- Audit rights
- Subcontractor requirements
- Breach liability

### 4.2 Service Level Agreements (SLA)
- Availability requirements
- Response time targets
- Escalation procedures
- Performance metrics
- Penalty clauses for breaches

### 4.3 Data Protection Addendum
- Data processing terms
- Data subject rights support
- Deletion/return procedures
- Encryption requirements
- Compliance with regulations

---

## 5. Continuous Monitoring

### 5.1 Monitoring Schedule
- Annual security assessment
- Quarterly risk review
- Regular compliance updates
- Incident notification response

### 5.2 Monitoring Activities
- Vulnerability scanning results review
- Penetration test results
- Certification renewal verification
- Incident history review
- Performance metrics monitoring

### 5.3 Risk Scoring
Each vendor receives risk score based on:
- Criticality of service
- Access level to systems
- Data sensitivity
- Security maturity
- Incident history

---

## 6. Incident Management

### 6.1 Incident Notification
- Within 24 hours of discovery
- Details: what, when, impact
- Containment actions taken
- Remediation plan
- Root cause analysis

### 6.2 Incident Investigation
- Participate in investigation
- Provide forensic evidence
- Full cooperation expected
- Timeline documentation
- Corrective action plan

### 6.3 Incident Response
- Assess impact on our organization
- Determine customer notification requirements
- Support communication with customers
- Monitor for recurrence

---

## 7. Sub-Processor Management

### 7.1 Sub-Processor Approval
- Notification before engagement
- Security assessment required
- Same requirements as primary vendor
- Right to audit

### 7.2 Sub-Processor Terms
- Equivalent security obligations
- Data protection requirements
- Audit rights
- Incident notification

---

## 8. Access Management

### 8.1 System Access
- Minimal access necessary only
- Time-limited credentials
- Regular access review
- Immediate removal upon termination
- Authentication required for all access

### 8.2 Data Access
- Limited to necessary data
- Purpose-specific access
- Data sensitivity classification respected
- Audit trail maintained
- Encryption required

---

## 9. Termination and Off-boarding

### 9.1 Contract Termination
- Formal termination notice
- Transition plan required
- Knowledge transfer provided
- Timeline for system access removal
- Data handling procedures

### 9.2 Off-boarding Checklist
- [ ] System access revoked
- [ ] VPN credentials disabled
- [ ] Equipment returned
- [ ] Data returned or destroyed
- [ ] Contract closure documentation
- [ ] Lessons learned captured

---

## 10. Vendor Renewal

### 10.1 Annual Review
- Assessed against current criteria
- Contract renewal consideration
- Risk reassessment
- Performance evaluation
- Updated security assessment

### 10.2 Renewal Decision
- Renewal recommended
- Renewal with conditions
- Non-renewal and transition plan
- Alternative vendor identification

---

## 11. Critical Vendor Management

### 11.1 Critical Vendor Definition
- Essential to operations
- No readily available alternative
- Significant access/data exposure
- High complexity

### 11.2 Critical Vendor Requirements
- Quarterly risk assessment
- Semi-annual business continuity review
- Enhanced audit rights
- Insurance verification
- Contingency planning

---

## 12. Governance and Oversight

- Vendor management registry maintained
- Risk assessment documented
- Contracts centrally stored
- Audit trail of all interactions
- Regular management reporting
