# Data Protection & Privacy Policy

**[ORGANIZATION_NAME]**

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes requirements for protecting personal data and ensuring compliance with GDPR, CCPA, and other privacy regulations.

---

## 2. Scope

All processing of personal data, including collection, storage, transfer, and deletion.

---

## 3. Data Classification

- **Personal Data:** Any information relating to identified or identifiable natural person
- **Sensitive Data:** Health, genetic, biometric data, race, religion, political affiliation, union membership, sexual orientation
- **Special Category Data:** Requires highest level of protection

---

## 4. Data Collection

### 4.1 Legal Basis
- Explicit consent from data subject
- Contract necessity
- Legal obligation
- Vital interests protection
- Public task performance
- Legitimate interests

### 4.2 Collection Principles
- Lawful and transparent
- Purpose-specified
- Data-minimization
- Accuracy requirement
- Storage limitation

---

## 5. Data Subject Rights

### 5.1 Right of Access
- Respond to access requests within 30 days
- Provide copy in electronic format
- No unreasonable delay
- Free of charge (except duplicates)

### 5.2 Right to Rectification
- Correct inaccurate personal data
- Complete incomplete data
- Implement within 30 days

### 5.3 Right to Erasure
- "Right to be forgotten" honored when applicable
- Data securely deleted
- Third parties notified
- Limited exceptions documented

### 5.4 Right to Data Portability
- Provide data in machine-readable format
- Transfer to another controller
- Request provided within 30 days

### 5.5 Right to Object
- Withdraw consent at any time
- Opt-out of processing
- Opt-out of marketing

---

## 6. Data Processing Agreements

### 6.1 Processor Agreements
All processors must have written agreements including:
- Confidentiality obligations
- Security requirements
- Sub-processor authorization
- Data subject rights support
- Audit rights

### 6.2 Joint Controller Agreements
When sharing data controller responsibility:
- Roles clearly defined
- Responsibilities allocated
- Contact information provided

---

## 7. Data Security

### 7.1 Encryption
- Personal data encrypted in transit (TLS 1.2+)
- Sensitive data encrypted at rest (AES-256)
- Encryption keys managed securely
- Key rotation annual minimum

### 7.2 Access Controls
- Least privilege principle
- Authentication required
- Multi-factor authentication for sensitive data
- Access logged and monitored

### 7.3 Incident Response
- Incident detection within 72 hours
- GDPR breach notification if applicable
- Data subject notification (if high risk)
- Authority notification (within 72 hours)
- Investigation and remediation

---

## 8. International Data Transfers

### 8.1 Approved Transfer Mechanisms
- Adequacy decisions (EU recognized)
- Standard contractual clauses
- Binding corporate rules
- Appropriate supplementary measures

### 8.2 Transfer Impact Assessment
- Legality of transfer evaluated
- Recipient country laws reviewed
- Additional safeguards identified
- Transfer approved before proceeding

---

## 9. Data Retention

### 9.1 Retention Schedules
- Personal data retained only as long as necessary
- Purpose-specific retention periods
- Regular review and deletion
- Audit trail maintained

### 9.2 Deletion Process
- Data securely destroyed
- Copies identified and deleted
- Third parties notified
- Destruction verified

---

## 10. Privacy Impact Assessments (PIA)

### 10.1 When Required
- New processing activities
- Technology changes
- Organizational changes
- Regulatory changes

### 10.2 Assessment Content
- Processing description
- Necessity and proportionality
- Risk analysis
- Mitigation measures
- Accountability demonstration
