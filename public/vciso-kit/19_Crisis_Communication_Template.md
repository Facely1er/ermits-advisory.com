## Crisis Communication Governance

### Activation Triggers
- **CRITICAL Incident**: Immediate crisis communication activation
- **HIGH Incident** with external impact: Activate within 1 hour
- **HIGH Incident** with potential media coverage: Activate immediately
- **Data breach** affecting customers/public: Immediate activation

## Internal Communication

### Phase 1: Immediate (0-2 hours)

#### All-Hands Message
**Subject**: [INCIDENT TYPE] - Crisis Team Activated

**Draft**:
```
Dear [ORGANIZATION_NAME] Team,

The company's security and IT teams have identified [BRIEF DESCRIPTION OF INCIDENT]
at [TIME] on [DATE].

We are taking this matter seriously and have:
- [ACTION 1]: Activated our crisis response team
- [ACTION 2]: Isolated affected systems
- [ACTION 3]: Engaged law enforcement (if applicable)

WHAT WE KNOW:
- [FACT 1]
- [FACT 2]

WHAT WE DON'T YET KNOW:
- [UNKNOWN 1]
- [UNKNOWN 2]

WHAT WE'RE DOING:
- [RESPONSE STEP 1]
- [RESPONSE STEP 2]

WHAT YOU SHOULD DO:
- [INSTRUCTION 1] - e.g., "Change your password"
- [INSTRUCTION 2] - e.g., "Do not discuss outside the company"
- [INSTRUCTION 3] - e.g., "Report suspicious activity"

Updates will be provided [FREQUENCY].

Questions? Email [CONTACT] or call [PHONE].

[EXECUTIVE SIGNATURE]
```

#### Talking Points for Employees
- "This incident was discovered [WHEN] and is being actively investigated"
- "Our priority is containing the situation and protecting data"
- "We are notifying customers only if they have been directly impacted"
- "We appreciate your patience as we work through this"
- "Do not speculate or discuss external details on social media"

### Phase 2: Customer Service Team Briefing

**Talking Points for Customer Support**:
```
IF CUSTOMERS ASK ABOUT [INCIDENT]:

"[ORGANIZATION_NAME] has identified a security incident that
occurred on [DATE] at [TIME].

[DESCRIBE INCIDENT - Keep simple and non-technical]

Here's what you need to know:
1. [FACT 1 - e.g., "Your account password was not compromised"]
2. [FACT 2 - e.g., "We are monitoring for fraud"]
3. [FACT 3 - e.g., "You don't need to take immediate action"]

Here's what we're doing:
- [ACTION 1]
- [ACTION 2]
- [ACTION 3]

If you have concerns, please contact [SUPPORT CONTACT]
or visit [WEBSITE] for more information.

Thank you for your patience."
```

### Phase 3: Direct Customer Notification (If Required)

**Email Template**:
```
Subject: [ORGANIZATION_NAME] Security Notification - Action May Be Required

Dear Valued Customer,

We are writing to inform you of a security incident that has
affected [BRIEF DESCRIPTION].

WHAT HAPPENED:
[2-3 sentences explaining incident in plain language]

WHAT INFORMATION WAS AFFECTED:
[List: Names, Emails, Phone Numbers, etc. - Be specific]

WHAT WE'VE DONE:
[Describe response actions]

WHAT YOU SHOULD DO:
[Provide specific action items, e.g. change password, monitor account]

WHAT WE'RE OFFERING:
[Credit monitoring, identity protection service details]

MORE INFORMATION:
Visit [WEBSITE] or call [PHONE] for details.

We apologize for this incident and any inconvenience it may cause.

[SIGNATURE]
[ORGANIZATION_NAME]
```

### Spokesperson Guidelines

**Approved Spokespersons**:
- [ ] CEO
- [ ] Chief Communications Officer
- [ ] Legal Counsel (for legal questions)
- [ ] CISO (for technical questions)

**Spokesperson Rules**:
- Only approved spokespersons speak to media
- All media requests go through Communications Director
- Verify journalist identity before responding
- Stay on message
- Don't speculate
- Don't disclose investigation details

### Message Control

**KEY MESSAGES** (Repeat in all communications):
1. "[ORGANIZATION_NAME] takes security seriously"
2. "We identified the incident and responded immediately"
3. "We are fully cooperating with authorities"
4. "Customer trust is our priority"

**DO NOT SAY**:
- [ ] Details of the attack vector
- [ ] Names of tools used by attackers
- [ ] Speculation on motives
- [ ] Comparison to other breaches
- [ ] Timelines we're not certain about
- [ ] Negative statements about customers

## Scenario-Specific Communications

### SCENARIO 1: Data Breach with Customer Impact

**Initial Message**:
"[ORGANIZATION_NAME] has identified a security incident in which
customer data may have been accessed. We have [IMMEDIATE ACTIONS]
and are investigating fully."

**Customer Letter**:
[Use data breach template above]

**Media Statement**:
[Emphasize response and customer protection]

**Employee Talking Points**:
"This is being treated with utmost seriousness. We are notifying
affected customers and offering [PROTECTION SERVICE]."

### SCENARIO 2: Ransomware Attack

**Initial Message**:
"[ORGANIZATION_NAME] has identified a ransomware attack affecting
[SYSTEMS]. We have activated our incident response plan and have
[NOT/WILL NOT] paid any ransom."

**Key Points**:
- "Systems are being restored"
- "Backups were unaffected"
- "Normal operations resuming [TIMELINE]"
- "Law enforcement is investigating"

### SCENARIO 3: Credential Compromise

**Initial Message**:
"[ORGANIZATION_NAME] has identified that [X] user credentials may
have been compromised. We are [IMMEDIATE ACTIONS] and recommend customers
[ACTIONS NEEDED]."

**Key Points**:
- "Reset your password immediately"
- "Enable multi-factor authentication"
- "Monitor your account for unauthorized activity"
- "We are monitoring for fraud"

### SCENARIO 4: Denial of Service (OutagePublic Impact)

**Initial Message**:
"[ORGANIZATION_NAME]'s services are temporarily unavailable due to
a security attack. We are working to restore service and expect
availability [ESTIMATED TIME]."

**Updates**:
Every 30 minutes until service restored:
- Current status
- Estimated restoration time
- What we're doing
- Customer action items (if any)
