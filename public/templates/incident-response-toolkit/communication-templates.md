# Incident Response Communication Templates

## Overview
This document contains pre-approved communication templates for various stakeholder groups during and after a security incident.

---

## Template Index

1. **Internal Notifications**
   - Executive Leadership Notification
   - Employee Notification
   - IT Department Alert

2. **External Notifications**
   - Customer Breach Notification
   - Regulatory Notification
   - Partner/Vendor Notification

3. **Media & Public Relations**
   - Initial Public Statement
   - Follow-up Statement
   - FAQ Document

4. **Legal & Regulatory**
   - Law Enforcement Notification
   - Breach Notification (GDPR)
   - Breach Notification (HIPAA)
   - SEC Disclosure

## 2. External Notifications

### 2.1 Customer Breach Notification

**Subject:** Important Security Notice Regarding Your [ORGANIZATION] Account

Dear [CUSTOMER NAME],

We are writing to inform you of a security incident that may have affected your personal information.

**What Happened**
On [DATE], [ORGANIZATION NAME] discovered [DESCRIPTION OF INCIDENT]. We immediately launched an investigation with the assistance of leading cybersecurity experts and notified law enforcement.

Our investigation determined that an unauthorized party accessed our systems between [DATE RANGE] and may have accessed the following types of information:

- [DATA TYPE 1 - e.g., Names]
- [DATA TYPE 2 - e.g., Email addresses]
- [DATA TYPE 3 - e.g., Encrypted passwords]

**What Information Was Involved**
Based on our investigation, the following information related to your account may have been accessed:
- [SPECIFIC DATA TYPES FOR THIS CUSTOMER]

**What Information Was NOT Involved**
- [EXPLICITLY STATE WHAT WAS NOT COMPROMISED - e.g., Credit card numbers, Social Security numbers]

**What We Are Doing**
We take the security of your information very seriously. In response to this incident, we have:
- Implemented additional security measures to protect your information
- Engaged leading cybersecurity firms to investigate and secure our systems
- Reported this incident to law enforcement and regulatory authorities
- Reset passwords for all affected accounts (if applicable)

**What You Can Do**
While we have no evidence that your information has been misused, we recommend you take the following precautionary steps:

1. **Change Your Password**
   - If you used the same password on other sites, change those as well
   - Use a strong, unique password for each account
   - Enable two-factor authentication where available

2. **Monitor Your Accounts**
   - Review your account statements for unauthorized activity
   - Report any suspicious activity to your financial institution immediately

3. **Be Alert for Phishing**
   - Be cautious of unsolicited communications asking for personal information
   - Verify the sender before clicking links or opening attachments
   - [ORGANIZATION] will never ask you to provide sensitive information via email

4. **Credit Monitoring** (if applicable)
   - We are offering [X] months of complimentary credit monitoring services
   - Enrollment instructions: [DETAILS]
   - Enrollment deadline: [DATE]

**For More Information**
We have established a dedicated response line to answer your questions:
- **Phone:** [TOLL-FREE NUMBER] (Available [HOURS])
- **Email:** [DEDICATED EMAIL ADDRESS]
- **Website:** [DEDICATED FAQ PAGE]

We sincerely apologize for this incident and any concern it may cause. Protecting your information is one of our highest priorities, and we are committed to preventing incidents like this in the future.

Sincerely,

[NAME]
[TITLE]
[ORGANIZATION NAME]

## Communication Approval Process

**All external communications must be approved by:**
1. Legal Counsel
2. Chief Information Security Officer
3. Chief Communications Officer
4. Chief Executive Officer (for major incidents)

**Approval Workflow:**
1. Draft communication using appropriate template
2. Submit to Legal for review
3. Submit to CISO for technical accuracy
4. Submit to Communications for messaging review
5. Obtain CEO approval if required
6. Document all approvals before release

**Response Time Guidelines:**
- Internal notifications: 1-2 hours
- Customer notifications: 24-72 hours (or per regulatory requirements)
- Public statements: Review and approve within 4 hours of decision to release

---

## Template Customization Guidelines

1. **Review annually** and after each incident
2. **Customize** with organization-specific information
3. **Pre-approve** templates with Legal and Communications
4. **Test** templates during tabletop exercises
5. **Update** contact information quarterly
6. **Train** incident response team on proper use
